Currently on the network

Locomotives

Irish Rail

201 Class

The 201 Class locomotives are the newest and most powerful locomotives operating in Ireland. They are now in use on the Dublin-Cork train hauling MK4 carriages, the Enterprise hauling DD coaches and occasional freight workings.

071 Class

The 071 Class locomotives are in use for freight trains, shunting and the occasional railtour. NIR own 3 of these locomotives but they are called the 110 class up north.

Coaches

Mark IV

The Mark IV coaches were built by CAF between 2004-2005 to replace the Mark 3 and Mark 2 coaches operating on the Dublin - Cork train. They operate in 8 car push pull sets hauled by the 201 class and featuring a control car at the other end to shortern turn around times

De Deitrich

The De Dietrich coaches operate the cross-border Enterprise service between Dublin Connolly and Belfast. They were built by De Dietrich Ferroviaire in France and introduced into service in 1997. They were designed with interiors very simillar to early Eurostar stock.
